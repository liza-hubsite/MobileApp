Inside your body

On day 3 of your cycle, your uterus is continuing to shed its thick lining. Your uterus assists in the removal this lining by making small muscle contractions, which can cause cramps or lower back pain. Hormone levels, especially estrogen and progesterone, are still low at this point. Because of this, you may feel tired, more emotional than usual, or low on energy. At the same time, your brain is already sending signals to the ovaries to begin preparing for the next cycle, and several tiny sacs in your ovaries, each containing an immature egg, are starting to grow and develop.

Day 3 Action Plan

Low-Impact Movement

Gentle movement like light yoga, stretching, or short walks can help soothe lower back discomfort and release tension. These activities may support flexibility, circulation, and relaxation.

Iron-Rich Food

Eating iron-rich foods supports healthy red blood cells and energy. Good sources include meat, beans, lentils, and leafy greens. Pair with vitamin C foods to improve absorption.
